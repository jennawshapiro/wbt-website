Don't Skip the Convo About Feelings at Your Next Team Retreat

Talking about feelings at work isn't a distraction — it's a leadership advantage.

Talking about feelings at team retreats unlocks creativity, purpose, and culture — despite many leaders' instinct to skip these conversations in the name of efficiency.

Why It's Smart to Make Space for Feelings

Modern workplaces ask that people feel valued, heard, and aligned with a collective mission. Generational differences can create disconnects: some leaders operate from traditional workplace values — hard work leading to advancement — while many workers prioritize work-life balance and seek meaning beyond compensation.

The nonprofit sector faces a particular version of this. Staff bring personal passion and conviction to social change work, yet experience real emotional and sensory overload when addressing systemic problems while the world feels chaotic. Leaders who are unprepared to navigate these emotional currents often shut the conversations down — which only deepens isolation.

How to Let Feelings Into Team Engagements

The solution requires ongoing commitment, not a one-time conversation. Leaders create sustained space for acknowledging feelings, then shift toward action: building consensus around how the team wants to show up together.

Leaders aren't responsible for managing employees' emotions, but they can honor the diverse experiences and feelings people hold about workplace dynamics. Facilitated retreats that treat each individual as a whole human — not just a set of accountabilities — help teams access their creative potential.
