Choosing the Culture We Build on Campaigns

Team and leadership development in a statewide coordinated political campaign.

Service
Team Coaching

The team as the unit of change

Background

A statewide coordinated campaign team aligns and executes shared voter contact, organizing, and turnout efforts across all Democratic candidates and races in a state. The Democratic National Committee partnered with Work Better Together to pilot leadership development programming with the Virginia Coordinated Campaign, focused on Regional Organizing Directors and Deputy Organizing Directors. The dual goal was supporting these leaders as people and managers during an intense cycle, while gathering learnings for future campaign teams.

The Challenge

Middle managers carried significant emotional and operational load in a high-stress, rapidly scaling environment. Team culture was being shaped in real time under extreme pressure. The team wanted space to reflect, connect, and build resilience — though timing and system-level constraints complicated the effort, and there was a risk that insight would not translate into sustained behavior change without intentional follow-through.

Our Approach

We designed and facilitated a multi-modal leadership development pilot combining retreats, virtual learning, and coaching-informed practices.

Leadership retreats

A virtual DOD retreat and an in-person regional retreat focused on emotional intelligence, stress management, team culture, and actionable commitments — creating rare space for leaders to step out of constant execution mode.

Team culture & resilience workshops

Leaders explored Circles of Control and Influence, managing up and across, and the impact of stress on leadership behavior — grounded in real campaign dynamics rather than abstract theory.

Iterative design & deep listening

Session flow and content adapted in real time based on participant feedback, spending more time where folks explicitly asked for support — notably around stress, joy, and self-management.

What Shifted

The most powerful impact came from in-person experiences, after which the team reported stronger relationships and deeper alignment, increased awareness of how personal stress affects team culture, renewed commitment to centering joy and assuming positive intent, and greater clarity about individual growth edges as managers. The engagement also surfaced important constraints: virtual sessions were less effective given campaign timing, and leaders wanted more role-specific spaces.
