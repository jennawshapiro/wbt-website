Leadership Coaching1:1 Coaching

Developing Endurance and Agency

1:1 coaching with a senior leader to navigate relational tension.

Service
Leadership Coaching

1:1 coaching for individual leaders

Background

A senior leader at a mid-sized nonprofit engaged coaching while managing strain in their relationship with an Executive Director — visionary, but with blunt communication and limited awareness of impact. The client's core question was: “How do I lead well in this context without losing myself, or the people I care about?”

The Challenge

The client navigated a power dynamic where honest feedback felt risky, recurring interpersonal tension, and growing burnout — along with the question of whether that stress was even necessary. Previous attempts at adaptation hadn't resolved the fundamental tension between values and reality.

Our Approach

Over 12 sessions, coaching focused on three interconnected areas.

From reaction to reflection

Creating space to identify patterns and shift from reactive to strategic thinking.

Practicing courageous, regulated communication

Using sessions as a laboratory to practice difficult conversations, test approaches, and integrate learning through cycles of experiment, reflection, and application.

Expanding the frame

Shifting from individual coping to organizational health — advocating for shared values and working norms.

Outcomes and Shifts

The client developed greater self-trust, clarity about boundaries, more direct conversations with leadership, and the courage to name patterns affecting morale. They moved from endurance to agency — no longer absorbing stress as inevitable.

Leadership growth doesn't come from working harder or learning the 'right' script. It comes from creating space to reflect, practice, and act with intention.
