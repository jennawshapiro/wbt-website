Repairing a Ruptured Relationship

2:2 coaching to repair a manager-employee relationship.

The Partnership

An organization engaged Work Better Together to support a manager and a direct report experiencing strain. Both were capable and mission-committed, but tension, misalignment, and mistrust were beginning to undermine performance. We used 2:2 Coaching, informed by the Cape Cod Model, emphasizing awareness, possibility, and choice.

The Challenge

The relationship featured recurring cycles of misunderstanding and defensiveness:

  • Unclear, misaligned expectations causing frustration.
  • Feedback experienced as threatening rather than developmental.
  • Questioned intentions and assumed criticism.
  • Anxiety replacing trust and open problem-solving.

Both parties wanted a safer, clearer relationship built on good intent and openness.

Our Approach

The multi-session engagement worked with each person individually and together, focusing on awareness, pattern interruption, and new relational norms through surfacing goals and tensions openly, building shared language via a Cycle for Repairing Harm, shifting from blame to accountability, practicing productive conversations using advocacy and inquiry, and making small, visible commitments.

Outcomes

After six 90-minute sessions, both reported increased clarity around expectations and communication norms, reduced fear replaced by curiosity and generous interpretation, and consistent feedback routines that surfaced challenges early.

Why It Matters

Interpersonal conflict between two people can take out an entire team. Investment in rupture repair transforms tension into trust, and misalignment into shared capacity.
